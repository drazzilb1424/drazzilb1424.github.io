FAQ

Why use a middleman?
  • Grading your cards doesn’t have to be a headache. All you need to do is choose the cards, get them ready, and send them to me—simple as that.
  • Most grading companies make you pay for a membership before you can even submit, but using a middleman means you can skip that cost entirely.
  • The submission process itself can be time-consuming and confusing—between picking the correct label, filling out forms, and making sure your cards are packaged just right, there’s plenty of room for mistakes. I handle all of that so you don’t have to stress.
  • And let’s not forget shipping. Insurance and postage fees can add up quickly, but when submissions are grouped together through a middleman, the costs are shared—helping you save significantly on shipping.

How should I prep my order?
  • Cards must be in a penny sleeve and a CardSaver (semi-rigid holder) as required by PSA. If you send them in top loaders, we’ll re-case them into semi-rigids for safe transit and PSA processing, with a $0.25 fee per card.
  • We’ll record card details and set a max insured value, but you should also research to choose the right service level. PSA may upcharge if a card’s value exceeds the tier limit, and any extra fees are your responsibility. If this happens, we’ll notify you beforehand and update your invoice.
  • You have the option to add insurance on shipments to PSA by specifying the coverage amount. USPS insurance rates and details are available on their website.

How much is return shipping?
For orders being shipped from us to you, there will be an additional $5 flat fee for this - no matter the order size. You will receive an email with tracking once we ship your slabs back to you. Expected turnaround time from us receiving the order from PSA to shipping to you will be no longer than two business days.

When do I pay for my submission?
Payment isn’t due until PSA has graded your cards and billed us—usually about 1 week before they’re shipped back to us. At this point, you’ll receive a Square invoice by email. Grades are shared upon request and only after payment is complete.

How should I ship my cards?
Ultimately, it’s up to you how you package your cards, but my priority is making sure they stay protected in transit. A safe method is to place the cards between two sturdy pieces of cardboard, secure them with rubber bands, then wrap the bundle generously in bubble wrap. From there, place the wrapped bundle inside a box for shipping.

What cards do you grade?
We accept all types of trading cards for grading—whether it’s Yu-Gi-Oh, Digimon, Dragon Ball, One Piece, or beyond. If PSA grades it, we’ll handle it for you. Our priority is making sure you’re satisfied with the process.
